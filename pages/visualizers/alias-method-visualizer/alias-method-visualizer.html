🎲 How the Table Actually Works

📏

Scale, Don't Normalize

Weights are scaled so their average is exactly 1 (multiply each probability by N). Items below 1 are "small" and need help filling their slot; items above 1 are "large" and have surplus probability to donate.

🔗

Pairing Small with Large

Each small item's slot is filled with its own probability plus a borrowed slice from a large item — recorded as that slot's "alias." The large item's remaining surplus is re-classified and the process repeats until every slot sums to exactly 1.

🪙

Sampling Is Just a Coin Flip

Pick a uniformly random slot i, then flip a coin weighted by prob[i]. Heads keeps item i; tails redirects to alias[i]. No scanning, no cumulative sum walk — exactly one array lookup and one comparison.

🎮

Real Deployments

Game engines use this for loot drop tables sampled millions of times per session. Load balancers use it to pick weighted backend servers. ML negative-sampling layers (like word2vec) use alias tables to draw from skewed vocabulary frequency distributions at scale.

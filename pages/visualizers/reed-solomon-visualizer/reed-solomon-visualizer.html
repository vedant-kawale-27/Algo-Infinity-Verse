The Hard Mathematical Boundary

Fail exactly M disks: reconstruction always succeeds, no matter which ones. Fail M+1 disks: reconstruction is mathematically impossible — not "harder," genuinely impossible. Try it.

💿 Why Points on a Curve Work

📈

A Line Needs 2 Points

You need exactly 2 points to uniquely determine a straight line — any 2 will do. A degree-(K-1) polynomial works the same way but needs K points. This is the entire idea generalized: encode K data values as K points on a degree-(K-1) curve.

➕

Parity = More Points, Same Curve

Generate M extra points on that exact same curve — these are the parity blocks. They carry no new information individually, but any K points total (data or parity, mixed) are enough to reconstruct the whole curve via Lagrange interpolation.

🔧

RAID 6's Real Mechanism

RAID 6 stores 2 parity blocks per stripe using exactly this math, tolerating any 2 simultaneous disk failures. This is why RAID 6 arrays can survive a second disk dying during the rebuild from the first failure — a real, common failure mode RAID 5 cannot survive.

📱

QR Codes Use This Too

A QR code's error correction level (L/M/Q/H) directly controls how many Reed-Solomon parity symbols are embedded — which is exactly why a QR code with a logo stamped over part of it, or a scratched corner, still scans correctly.

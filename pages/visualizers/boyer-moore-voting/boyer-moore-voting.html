🗳️ Boyer-Moore Voting Explained

⚡

The Voting Intuition

Think of elements as voters and the current candidate as a surviving champion. Every mismatch is a "cancellation" — one vote for and one against annihilate each other. Because the majority appears more than n/2 times, it can absorb all cancellations and still survive.

🔄

Counter Hits Zero

When the counter reaches 0, it means equal votes for and against have cancelled. The next element becomes the new candidate with counter = 1. The majority element may or may not be the current candidate at this moment — it doesn't matter, it will win again.

✅

Verification is Mandatory

Boyer-Moore only guarantees: "IF a majority exists, this is it." It does NOT guarantee a majority exists. A no-majority sequence still outputs a candidate. A second O(n) pass counting actual occurrences is required to confirm count > n/2.

🏆

n/3 Extension

Track two candidates and two counters. When an element matches neither candidate and both counters are positive, decrement both. Elements appearing more than n/3 times survive. Used in LeetCode 229 — Majority Element II.

🛰️ Kalman Filter Explained

⏩

Predict Step

Using the motion model, propagate the current state estimate forward in time. Mean stays on the expected trajectory; variance P grows because without new data, uncertainty can only increase. x̂⁻ = x̂, P⁻ = P + Q.

🎯

Update Step

A new measurement z arrives. Kalman gain K = P⁻/(P⁻ + R) decides how much to trust it. New estimate = prediction + K × (measurement − prediction). Uncertainty shrinks: P = (1 − K) × P⁻.

⚖️

Kalman Gain Intuition

K lives in [0, 1]. K≈0 means R is huge relative to P — the sensor is very noisy, trust the prediction almost completely. K≈1 means P is huge relative to R — you've been predicting a long time without data, trust the measurement almost completely.

🚀

Why It's Optimal

Under Gaussian noise assumptions, the Kalman Filter produces the minimum mean squared error (MMSE) estimate — no other linear estimator can do better. It powered Apollo mission guidance, modern GPS receivers, drone PID stabilization, and radar tracking.

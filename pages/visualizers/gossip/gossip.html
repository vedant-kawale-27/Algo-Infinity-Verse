Epidemic Gossip Engine

No leader. No census. Just rumor, spreading exponentially.

With no central coordinator, nodes in systems like DynamoDB or Cassandra discover state and failures through gossip — every round, each node swaps its state matrix with a random peer. State converges in O(log n) rounds. Failure isn't a boolean either: a φ-accrual detector converts a sliding window of heartbeat gaps into a live probability that a node is dead.
